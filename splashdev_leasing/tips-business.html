Is a line of credit for you?

A line of credit is a loan facility that can readily be drawn down or repaid back at any time within a pre-determined limit. This key feature means a line of credit behaves much like a credit card or an overdraft, but rather than being linked to a card or a bank account, it is its own standalone facility.

Like a credit card or an overdraft, interest is only charged on any amount that is drawn and the funds can be drawn down or repaid back at any time. Your company can get from $2,000 to $250,000+ with a line of credit.
What are the advantages and disadvantages of a line of credit?

These are the advantages of a line of credit: super flexible, only pay interest on funds used, funding available when needed, suitable for a multiple purposes, no security for small balances, great way to build credit score, and simple application process. These are the disadvantages of a line of credit: may require long trading history, can include unexpected fees, may require security, higher rates for lower credit, and may require director’s guarantee.

What is an equipment loan or chattel mortgage?

Think of this as a regular loan, where the lender gives you cash to buy an asset and then takes that asset as security for if you fail to repay the loan. This is how an equipment loan or chattel mortgage works. The lender will give you the cash to purchase the equipment then use the equipment as security for the money you are borrowing from them.

What are the advantages and disadvantages of equipment loan or chattel mortgage?

Equipment loans and chattel mortgages are popular methods of financing new pieces of equipment. Chattel mortgages or equipment loans get favourable GST treatment – as you are making the full purchase upfront you can claim back any GST input credit in full at the time of purchase — this becomes especially valuable on larger purchases. But equipment loans often have higher credit quality requirements and can require more contribution upfront. Equipment loans also tend to have higher fees compared to hire-purchase or leases, as setting up the equipment loan costs the lender more time and documentation compared to the other types of equipment financing.
